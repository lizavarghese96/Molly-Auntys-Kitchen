🍱 The Menu

What is pothichoru?

Kerala's original packed lunch

Pothichoru is a traditional Kerala meal — rice, curries, pickle and sides — wrapped in banana leaf. The leaf subtly flavours the food and keeps it warm. It is comfort food, it is nostalgic, and there is nothing like it made fresh at home.

📋 The Plan

Week by week — what we do, when, and why.

The golden rule for April

No orders in April. Not a single one. April is only for setting things up so that May feels easy. This is not laziness — this is being smart about it.

April — Planning month
Week 1 · April 1–7

Set up the basics

Create the Instagram page. Decide the business name together. Set up a WhatsApp business number for orders. No posting yet — just building the foundation quietly.

Week 2 · April 8–14

Cook a test batch

Aunty cooks one full batch of all 4 types. Track every cost — rice, curry, fish, chicken, gas used, banana leaf. This gives the real number to price from. No guessing.

Week 3 · April 15–21

Photos, content, and pricing

Take proper photos in good natural light. Film a short reel of the packing. Write captions. Finalise menu prices based on the test batch cost. Set up UPI for payments.

Week 4 · April 22–30

Free trial with people we know

Give 3–5 pothichorus to neighbours or friends. Get honest feedback. Fix anything aunty wants to change. By April 30 we should feel confident and ready.

May — Soft launch
Week 1 · May 1–7

First post, first real orders

Post the reel and photos on Instagram and WhatsApp status. Tell friends and family. Maximum 5 orders per day only. Deliver, learn, fix anything that felt awkward.

Week 2 · May 8–14

Open to the local area

Post in Malleshpalya and Kaggadasapura Facebook groups. If Week 1 went well, increase to 8 orders per day. Ask first customers for a photo or WhatsApp review.

Weeks 3–4 · May 15–31

Find the rhythm

Track which dish sells most. Which day gets most orders. Note any delivery or gas problems. All this data shapes June's plan. We learn in May. We improve in June.

June onward
Flexible

Scale or adjust — based on what May taught us

If orders are healthy — slowly increase the daily cap. If a dish isn't selling — remove or reprice it. If a day is always slow — rest that day. There is no failure here, only learning.

How orders work — every day

Order rule: 8 PM the night before

Customers must order by 8 PM the evening before. No exceptions. This gives aunty time to check stock, buy ingredients if needed, and cook fresh and properly the next morning. Late orders = next day.

⚠️ Concerns

Things that could go wrong — and exactly how we handle each one.

Thinking ahead is not being negative

Knowing about a problem before it happens means we are not surprised by it. Every concern here has a plan. None of these should stop us from starting.

High priority
Gas shortage in Bangalore This is real and ongoing. Our whole business depends on cooking. No gas = no food.
✅ Always keep one backup cylinder at home. Never start cooking if gas is uncertain that morning. Take a day off rather than risk bad food. Post a "closed today" story — customers will understand.
Overcommitting orders Saying yes to 15 orders when aunty is comfortable with 8 means tired cooking and worse food.
✅ Aunty sets the daily cap. Post "sold out for today" when reached. This actually creates a sense of demand — scarcity is good for the brand.
Medium priority
Bangalore heat in May May is very hot. Fish spoils faster. Long deliveries in the afternoon heat are risky.
✅ Deliver within 2 hours of packing. Fish orders only for customers within 15–20 min delivery. Deliver before 1:30 PM before the heat peaks.
Delivery cost eating into profit Multiple small deliveries in different directions add up quickly.
✅ Charge a flat ₹30–40 delivery fee per order. Keep zone tight — Malleshpalya and Kaggadasapura only. Batch nearby orders into one delivery where possible.
Ingredient prices changing Fish prices especially can shift week to week.
✅ Review prices every 2–3 weeks. Build a buffer into fish pricing. If costs spike — update the menu and announce it honestly on Instagram. Customers respect transparency.
Worth knowing about
Slow order days at the start The first two weeks will likely be slow. That is completely normal.
✅ The goal of Week 1 is not profit — it is learning the process and getting the first few happy customers who will then tell others. Keep posting consistently.
A customer complains It will happen at some point. Even the best cooks get complaints.
✅ Respond calmly. Ask what was wrong. If valid — offer a replacement. Do not argue. One handled complaint can turn a critic into a loyal customer.
Your energy and mental health This should help, not hurt. Marketing is 30–45 minutes max per day.
✅ If a day is hard — put up a "closed today" story. The business will be there tomorrow. Your wellbeing comes before every single order.

The bottom line

None of these are dealbreakers. They are just things to keep an eye on. The fact that we have thought about them puts us ahead of most small businesses that start without any planning at all.
