The Plan

Week by week — what we do, when we do it, and why.

April
Planning month
May
Soft launch
June+
Grow or adjust

The golden rule for April

No orders in April. Not a single one. April is only for setting things up so that May feels easy and not overwhelming. This is not laziness — this is being smart.

April — Planning month
Week 1 · April 1–7

Set up the basics

Create an Instagram page. Decide on the business name together. Set up a WhatsApp business number for orders. No posting yet — just building the foundation.

Week 2 · April 8–14

Cook a test batch

Aunty cooks one full batch of all 4 types. Track every single cost — rice, curry, fish, chicken, egg, gas used, banana leaf / packaging. This gives us the real number to price from.

Week 3 · April 15–21

Photos, content, and pricing

Take proper photos of the pothichoru in good lighting. Film a short reel of the packing process. Write captions. Finalise the menu prices based on the test batch cost. Set up UPI for receiving payments.

Week 4 · April 22–30

Free trial run with people we know

Give 3–5 pothichorus to neighbours, friends, or family. Get honest feedback. Fix anything aunty wants to change. By April 30, we should feel ready to take real orders.

May — Soft launch
Week 1 · May 1–7

First post, first real orders

Post the reel and photos on Instagram and WhatsApp status. Tell friends and family directly. Maximum 5 orders per day only. Deliver, learn, and improve from any hiccups.

Week 2 · May 8–14

Open to the local area

Post in Malleshpalya and Kaggadasapura Facebook groups. If Week 1 went smoothly, increase to 8 orders per day. Ask first customers for a photo or a review — even a WhatsApp message is enough.

Weeks 3–4 · May 15–31

Find the rhythm

By now the routine will feel natural. Track which dish sells most. Track which day of the week gets the most orders. Note any problems with delivery or gas. All of this data shapes June's plan.

June onward
Flexible

Scale or adjust — based on what May taught us

If orders are healthy — slowly increase the daily maximum. If a particular dish is not selling — remove it or rethink the price. If a certain day is always slow — consider not taking orders that day. We learn in May, we improve in June.

The order rule — applies every single day

Orders must come in by 8 PM the night before

No exceptions. This gives aunty enough time to check what she has at home, buy anything extra if needed, and cook fresh and properly the next morning. Late orders will be politely declined and moved to the next day.

How delivery works

Delivery window  ·  11:30 AM to 1:30 PM (lunchtime)
Platform  ·  Rapido or Uber (whichever is cheaper that day)
Delivery zone  ·  Malleshpalya and Kaggadasapura only (to start)
Delivery cost  ·  Added to the order as a flat ₹30–40 fee
Payment  ·  UPI in advance — no cash, no pay on delivery

Marketing channels
Priority

Instagram

Reels of the packing process, food photos, daily stories showing what's available. Use Bangalore food hashtags.

Priority

WhatsApp status

Free daily marketing. Update status every morning with the menu. Forward to housing society groups and office contacts nearby.

Secondary

Facebook groups

Post in Malleshpalya and Kaggadasapura local groups. Reaches neighbours and the slightly older crowd who still use Facebook.

Secondary

Word of mouth

The first 10 customers are the most important. A happy customer will tell 5 people. Consider a small referral discount.
