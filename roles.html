Who does what

Clear roles from day one. No confusion, no stepping on each other's toes.

MT

Molly aunty

The cook — the heart of this business
  • Cook all orders fresh every morning
  • Decide what goes inside each pothichoru
  • Keep ingredients stocked and fresh
  • Monitor and manage gas usage
  • Maintain taste and quality every day
  • Say clearly if any day she cannot cook
  • Decide the maximum orders she can handle
You

You

Marketing, orders, and operations
  • Run Instagram, WhatsApp, Facebook pages
  • Collect and confirm all orders by 8 PM each night
  • Tell aunty the exact order count each evening
  • Collect UPI payments before each order
  • Book Rapido or Uber each morning for delivery
  • Track every expense and profit each week
  • Handle any customer questions or issues
The daily routine together
By 8 PM
(previous night)
You collect all orders from WhatsApp and Instagram DM. You confirm the count with aunty. Collect UPI payment in advance from every customer.
7–9 AM
Aunty starts cooking. You book the delivery on Rapido or Uber based on the delivery addresses for the day.
10–11 AM
Aunty packs the pothichorus. You coordinate the pickup time with the delivery person.
11:30–1:30 PM
Deliveries go out. You track each delivery and confirm receipt with the customer via WhatsApp.
Evening
Post one Instagram story. Check DMs for next day orders. Update expense log. Rest. Maximum 30–45 minutes of marketing work.
The shared responsibilities

Weekly money review  ·  Both of us sit together every Sunday to review the week's income and expenses and split the profit.
Sourcing ingredients  ·  Aunty tells you what is needed. You help buy if she needs it, or she goes herself — whatever works that week.
Feedback from customers  ·  Any feedback you receive, you share with aunty. No filtering. Honest feedback makes the food better.

The rule that protects both of us

Aunty sets the limit. We respect it.

If aunty says she can comfortably cook 6 orders that day — the answer is 6 orders. Not 8 because we have demand. Not 10 because we want more money. Her kitchen, her pace, her call. The business is only sustainable if she is not exhausted doing it.

If you are having a bad day

Put up an Instagram story saying "closed today, back tomorrow." One missed day will not break this business. Your mental health is not negotiable either.
